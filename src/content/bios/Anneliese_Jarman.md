Anneliese is the Image Analyst at the Babraham Institute in Cambridge. She has a background in building microscopes for applications from fundamental biology to disease detection. The nuances of data from new microscopes, and the need to compare outputs with established imaging modalities, led her into image analysis with a focus on image quality and multimodal comparison in Fiji and MATLAB. A love of the craft has since expanded this to encompass quantitative analysis across a broad range of light microscopy techniques (including FLIM), on samples from whole organisms to subcellular structures, using diverse image analysis software and custom scripting.
